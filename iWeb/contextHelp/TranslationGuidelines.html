Translation Guidelines

What you are translating.

 

Whilst vivichart is a service, at its core  is a configurable software program. As such, there are more things happening behind the scenes to do with the configuration than may be apparent from looking at the Web Form that allows charts to be built. 

 

 

There are in the region of 600 words and terms that need to be translated for each language. 

  • A significant number of these are straightward - Yes, No, Blue, Red, Language.
  • Some are ambiguous - Column, Field, Lists. 
  • Some are semi technical - Node,  Linear, Gradient
  • A few are technical - Data Source, Tooltip, and
  • A few are terms - {0} does not appear to be a spreadsheet.

 

Assistance with the Translation

 

On-line contextual help is provided for each word/term. If further assitance is required, this can be requested online during the translation process by clicking on the Feedback about this form link that appears at the bottom of each page. 

 

Abbreviations

 

In certain cases, it will be necessary to abbrevaite words; normally due to the space available within a Web Form,

 

The width of the Content Area within the Web Forms is dynamic and will change automatically depending upon the text that appears within it.

However, you should try to keep all text as short as possible.

Consistency

 

You should be consistent in your use of the words that you translate.

 

 

 

Email Messages

 

There is no need to be brief in the translation of Email messages.

 

Simple Words

 

The foreign language words should be simple. For example, in English make better and improve would be prefereable to ameliorate. 

vivichart 's target market is administrative personnel rather than computing staff and the words and terms that are used should reflect that. It is likely that users of the service will know how to use Microsoft Word and, possibly, Microsoft Excel but not know much more about computing.
